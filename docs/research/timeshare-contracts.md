# Timeshare Contract Analyzer Framework

## Critical Statistics
- Average purchase: $24,140
- Average maintenance fee: $1,170-$1,480/year
- Annual fee increase: 3-8%
- 10-year fee increase: 42.34%
- Buyer regret rate: 85%
- Rescission usage: Only 15%

## Perpetuity Clauses - CRITICAL

### Detection
```
"This agreement shall be binding upon the Owner and Owner's heirs,
successors, and assigns in perpetuity..."
```

### Impact
- Obligations pass to heirs automatically
- Heirs have 9 months to file disclaimer with probate court
- Once any benefit received, disclaimer rights lost
- Each family member must file separately

## Maintenance Fee Traps

### Uncapped Increases
```
"Maintenance fees are subject to annual adjustment based on operating
costs and may increase without limitation..."
```

### Special Assessments
```
"The Association reserves the right to levy special assessments as
deemed necessary for capital improvements, repairs, or unforeseen expenses..."
```

### Real Examples (2025)
- Sedona Resort: $1,500/interval (2 years after $975 assessment)
- Honolulu: $1,200 "Supplemental Assessment"/interval

### Legal Precedent
PA Attorney General sued Split Rock Resort for exceeding 7.5% annual cap

## Exit Scam Indicators

### FTC Red Flags
1. Unsolicited contact offering exit help
2. Guarantees or promises to cancel
3. Large upfront fees
4. Fake affiliations with legitimate companies
5. Claims you cannot exit alone

### Major Scams
- $90 million scam (2022): Consumer Law Protection, Square One, Premier Reservations
- Minnesota (2025): $269,378 in refunds from Encore Law, Last Resort Consulting
- FBI: $50+ million lost in 2024

### Scam Language
- "We have a buyer ready for $XX,XXX"
- "Pay a small closing fee upfront"
- "Your timeshare is worth more than you paid"
- "Act now - offer expires today"

## Sales Misrepresentation

### "License to Lie" Clause
```
"This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.
No representations, warranties, or promises not contained herein shall
be binding..."
```

### Common Misrepresentations
1. "Your timeshare will appreciate" (investment claims)
2. "You can sell anytime for a profit" (easy resale)
3. "Maintenance fees will stay the same" (stable fees)
4. "You can easily rent your weeks" (rental income)
5. Financing bait-and-switch (0% promised, 15% delivered)

### Major Judgments
- Westgate (TN): $600K punitive for fraud
- FantaSea: $1.07M verdict
- Diamond (AZ): $800K settlement
- Wyndham (WI): $665K restitution

## Ownership Structures

### Deeded vs Right-to-Use

| Factor | Deeded | RTU |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Duration | Perpetual | 10-99 years |
| Recorded | Yes | No |
| Inheritance | Passes with obligations | Often reverts |
| Exit difficulty | Harder | Easier (wait out) |

## Legitimate Exit Options (Ranked)

1. **Rescission Period** - Free, 15% success rate
2. **Developer Deed-Back** - Wyndham Cares, Marriott, Hilton programs ($200-$1K)
3. **Resale Market** - RedWeek, TUG2 (expect $0-$1 sale)
4. **Timeshare Attorney** - $3K-$7K, for misrepresentation claims
5. **ARDA Exit Assistance** - (855) 939-1515

## Analyzer Checklist

### Financial Red Flags
- [ ] No cap on maintenance increases
- [ ] Unlimited special assessment authority
- [ ] Financing differs from verbal promises
- [ ] Hidden finance charges

### Exit Red Flags
- [ ] Perpetuity clause
- [ ] No termination provision
- [ ] Right of first refusal limiting resale
- [ ] No deed-back option

### Consumer Rights Red Flags
- [ ] Missing rescission disclosure
- [ ] Incorrect cancellation address
- [ ] Ambiguous rescission start date
